Before you sign the agreement

This is a legal agreement.

This agreement is a type of contract. It’s important you are happy with the details before you sign.

If you are unsure about any part of this agreement get advice from people you know or an organisation like Citizens Advice.

Check the details.

The wording of this agreement is based on the information you and the other side have discussed and agreed on. It has not been reviewed by the court.

You must decide if you want to sign this agreement.

What happens to the claim.

This agreement will put the claim on hold. The court won’t do anything more about this claim once this agreement is signed by those named. This is known as ‘a stay’.

If neither you or the other side contacts the court by the claim will be cancelled.

Once signed it's confidential.

Don’t tell anyone about this agreement after you’ve signed it. You must keep this information confidential..

You can ask people for advice before you sign it though.

If this agreement is broken

If the agreement is broken the Claimant can ask the court to either:

  • make a judgment for you for any money still owed under this agreement
  • restart the original claim for the full amount and arrange a hearing
