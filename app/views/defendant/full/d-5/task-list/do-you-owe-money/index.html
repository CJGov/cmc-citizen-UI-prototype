How do you respond to the claim?

Find out what each response means

Admit all of the claim

Pay immediately

If you admit all the claim you can pay the claimant within 14 days and you won’t get a CCJ.

Ask for more time

If you admit all the claim but need more time to pay, you can propose a timeline for repaying the claimant.

The claimant will decide whether to accept. If they do, they can ask the court to enter a CCJ against you and you’ll be sent an order to pay.

If they reject your offer, the court will decide how you should pay.

Admit part of the claim

If you admit part of the claim, the claimant decides whether to accept your part-admission.

If they accept, they can ask the court to enter a CCJ against you and you'll be sent an order to pay.

If they reject your part-admission, the case will proceed as a defended claim.

Reject all of the claim

You can reject the whole claim because you dispute it, or because you believe you've already paid the money.

You dispute the whole claim

If you dispute the whole claim, it may be transferred to a local County Court

The court will be the nearest one to your home or business if that’s a different court to the one where the claimant issued the claim.

You’ve paid the money already

If you believe you’ve already paid the money, the claimant will have to decide whether to proceed with the claim or withdraw it. They’ll tell the court and you within 28 days.

If the claimant decides to proceed, the case will proceed as a defended claim.
