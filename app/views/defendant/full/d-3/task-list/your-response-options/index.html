Your response options

Completing your claim

Admit all of the claim

If you admit all of the claim is fair you can contact the claimant yourself and pay the money. Ask them to let the court know when they have your payment.

If you can't afford to pay it all at once you can suggest a repayment plan for them to consider.

Admit part of the claim

If you think the claim is too high you can offer the amount you think you owe as part of your defence. You’ll get the chance to explain why your offer is the amount you believe you owe.

Reject the claim

If you don't accept you owe the amount of money claimed you can prepare and submit your defence online. You might have to go to a hearing - it can take up to 7 months to get a hearing date.


County Court Judgment (CCJ) - when you might get a CCJ

The claimant can ask for a CCJ to be made against you if you admit the claim. This is a ‘judgment by admission’ and means the court orders you to pay the claim.

Details of the CCJ will be entered on the Register of Judgments, Orders and Fines. Your name could stay on this Register for 6 years unless you pay the total amount you owe.

If you pay within 28 days

You can apply for a certificate of cancellation to get your name removed from the Register of Judgments, Orders and Fines using form N443 (opens in new window). This application costs £15.

If you pay after 1 month

You can apply for a certificate of satisfaction to get your debt marked as ‘satisfied’ in the Register of Judgments, Orders and Fines using form N443 (opens in new window). This application costs £15.

Your name will stay on the Register of Judgments, Orders and Fines for 6 years but people searching it will see that you’ve paid.

Reject the claim

If you reject the claim and lose your case, then you’ll get a CCJ ordering you to pay the full claim amount and any costs. Details of the CCJ will be entered on the Register of Judgments, Orders and Fines for 6 years.
