Respond to a money claim

You must respond to the money claim by [date].

[claimant name] can apply for a County Court Judgment (CCJ) against you if you don't respond in time. They can then use this to get the money from you. For example, they could hire bailiffs.

A CCJ also makes it harder for you to get things like a bank loan, credit card or mortgage.

Accept the money claim

If you accept the claim is fair you can contact [name of claimant] yourself and pay the money. Ask them to let the court know when they have your payment.

If you can't afford to pay it all at once you can suggest a repayment plan for them to consider.Reject the claim

If you don't accept you owe the amount of money claimed you can prepare and submit your defence online. You might have to go to a hearing - it can take up to 7 months to get a hearing date.

Make a deal

You can try to settle the claim by making [name of claimant] an offer. If [name of claimant] rejects your offer you’ll have to go a hearing. [name of claimant] can’t mention that you made them an offer at the hearing.

If you think you’re owed money

If you believe the claimant owes you money you can make a claim against them (‘counterclaim’). Download and fill in Defence and Counterclaim form N9B.

You might have to pay a court fee.

If you can't afford to pay the money back

You might be able to get free legal advice to help you deal with debt from organisations like StepChange.
