The company's full legal name

Watch out for

Non-UK companies

You can only claim against companies in England and Wales using this service. The ending of a company's full name can give a clue to a company's location - 'limited'. 'ltd' or 'plc' are common in the UK, whereas 'inc', 'GmbH', 'S.A.', 'B.V.' mean it is not a UK company.

Trading names

If your paperwork says, for example, "Saga Holidays is a trading name of ST&H Group Limited", then 'ST&H Group Limited' is the name you need to enter.

Franchises

A franchise is an individual store that's not owned by the parent brand. Large food or drink chains often operate franchises. For example KFC, McDonalds, Subway, Costa Coffee and Starbucks. Often some stores in the chain will be owned by the brand themself (eg 'McDonalds ltd') while others are owned by smaller independent businesses (eg 'ABC Stores Ltd'). You need to make the claim against the owner of the individual store.
