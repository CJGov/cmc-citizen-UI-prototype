State your version of events

We'll now ask you some questions and use your answers to make a 'witness statement'. This a document that sets out your view of the case.

A judge will read the witness statement - in your answers you should:

  • state facts, not opinions
  • be as clear as possible, especially about facts and evidence
  • include dates and a timeline if you can
  • stick to the details of this case
  • explain any differences between your view of the case and the defendant's
  • try to answer any questions a judge might have about the case
  • explain any jargon or technical language (use simple language if you can)
  • tell the truth
