Get the company's full legal name right


Warning Your claim will fail if you do not use the company's full legal name.

In most cases, the company's full legal name is not the name it is commonly known by.

The full legal name will almost always end in 'limited', 'ltd' or 'plc' and can sometimes be completely different to the name you know.

For example:

  • KFC is Kentucky Fried Chicken (Great Britain) Limited
  • Currys PC World is DSG Retail Limited
  • Easyjet is easyJet Airline Company Limited
  • British Gas is British Gas Trading Limited
  • Saga Holidays is ST&H Group Limited

Where to find the company's full legal name

It's often best to ask them, but you could also try the following.

Look at your paperwork

This could include quotes, estimates, contracts, invoices, receipts or terms and conditions.

These will often include the company's full name in a footer or small print, rather than the logo or heading.

The company's website

Websites often include legal documnts such as terms and conditions or privacy policies which state their full legal name.

You could also try entering the name you know the business as and 'terms and conditions' into a search engine.

Search Companies House

If you can't find the company's name on their documentation, you can search Companies House for free.

Try to match the registered office address and the employees listed with other information you have, to make sure you have the right business.
