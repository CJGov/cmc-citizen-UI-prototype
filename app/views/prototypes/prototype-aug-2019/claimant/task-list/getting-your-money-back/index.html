Making a claim doesn't guarantee getting money back

Even if you win your case or the defendant admits they owe money, you may not get the money back.

Consider their financial situation before you claim

Think about how likely it is that they'll ever have the money to pay you.

It'll be harder to get money back if they:

  • have no money or source of income
  • don't own anything they can sell to get the money
  • have a County Court Judgment (CCJ) or several of them (this suggests a history of unpaid debts)

You can search the Registry Trust to find out if a person or company has CCJs (opens in a new window). This costs £6 to £10.

If they have several CCJs against them, it might be less likely that you get paid.

How the court can help if you win

If you win, you don't get paid automatically but you can ask the court to:

  • give the defendant a CCJ which orders them to pay
  • order them to share details about their income and spending so you see what they can afford
  • send bailiffs to their home or business to see if anything can be sold to pay the debt
  • have money deducted from their wages
  • freeze assets or money in their account
  • order for any money from property or land sales to be used to pay the debt

Requesting a CCJ is free but the other requests cost an extra fee. They don't guarantee you get paid.

Find out more about how to enforce a CCJ (opens in a new window).
