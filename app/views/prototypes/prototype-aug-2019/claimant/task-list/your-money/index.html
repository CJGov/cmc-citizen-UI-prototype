Getting your money back

Even if you win your case, you may not get your money back.

The court can order the other party to pay you, using a County Court Judgment (CCJ) and there are methods of enforcing the CCJ (opens in a new window), such as the use of bailiffs, but these are not always successful.

Before making a claim you should consider:

  • do they have the money available to pay you?
  • do they have anything that could be sold to pay the debt?
  • do they have any existing CCJs?

You can search to find out if a company has any existing CCJs (opens in a new window) for a small fee.

If they have already have several judgments against them, you should consider whether they are able to pay you as well.
