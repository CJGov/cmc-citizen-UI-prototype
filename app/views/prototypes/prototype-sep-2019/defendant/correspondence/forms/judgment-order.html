How to remove a judgment from the register

Pay in full within one month

If you pay in full within one month or you've already paid, you can ask the court to remove the judgment from the register.

The judgment will be cancelled and won't appear in credit agency searches, though some agencies may not update their records immediately.

Pay in full after one month

If you pay in full after one month, you can ask the court to mark the judgment as satisfied.

Any credit agency that checks the register will see you've paid the money, though some may not update their records immediately.

The judgment will remain on the register for 6 years but it'll be marked as satisfied.

Get proof that a judgment is cancelled or satisfied

You can pay £15 to get a certificate that proves a judgment is cancelled or satisfied: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/form-n443-application-for-a-certificate-of-satisfaction-or-cancellation.

If you paid in full within one month or had already paid, you can apply for a certificate of cancellation.

If you paid in full after a month, you can apply for a certificate of satisfaction.

If you believe you don't owe the money

You can apply to the court to have them set aside the judgment. If the court agrees to do this, the judgment will be removed from your record.

To apply, complete this form and return it to the court: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/form-n244-application-notice.

You may have to pay a court fee of up to £255. You might be eligible to get help with court fees.

The court could ask you go to a hearing to explain why you don't owe the money.

If you don't go to the hearing, the court could reject your application and order you to pay the amount listed in this judgment.
