How mediation works

If both you and Jan Clark opt in, we'll contact you to arrange a phonecall with a mediator.

The mediator might need to speak to you more than once but the call won't last more than an hour.

A mediator will:

  • listen to each side separately - you won’t talk directly to the other side
  • ask you to consider what the other side says, but won’t make you compromise
  • help you reach an agreement, if possible

If mediation is successful we'll ask you to sign a contract to formalise the settlement. If anyone breaks the terms, the other party can apply to the court to enforce them.

If mediation fails and a hearing is needed, the mediation discussions can't be mentioned in court.
