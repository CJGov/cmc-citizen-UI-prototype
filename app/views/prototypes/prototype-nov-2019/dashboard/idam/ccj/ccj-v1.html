You have a County Court Judgment (CCJ)

{{ partyName( 'claimant' ) }} requested a CCJ against you because you didn't respond to their money claim.

What this means

The court believes you owe {{ partyName( 'claimant' ) }} £1,500.

The judgment explains how the court has ordered you to pay.

Download the CCJ

A CCJ may make it harder to get credit, such as a mobile phone contract, credit card or mortgage.

What are my options?

Settle the claim

If {{ partyName( 'claimant' ) }} tells us you've paid or agreed to settle the claim another way, the judgment will be marked as satisfied. It will stay on the register for 6 years.

If you don't believe you owe the money

Because you didn't respond in time, you can only dispute the claim by applying to cancel the CCJ.

The court will only cancel the CCJ if it accepts you didn't hear about the claim when {{ partyName( 'claimant' ) }} made it against you.

It costs £255 to ask the court to consider your request. You can't get the fee back, whether or not the court accepts your request.

If the court cancels the judgment

When you apply to cancel, we'll also ask you to submit a defence to the original claim.

If the court cancels the judgment, we'll ask {{ partyName( 'claimant' ) }} to respond to your defence.

If not, your defence will be cancelled. You'll have to pay {{ partyName( 'claimant' ) }} £1,500.

If you owe the money but can't pay now

You can ask to change when you pay {{ partyName( 'claimant' ) }}. You'll still owe £1,500 but you can ask to pay by a different date or in instalments.

You'll have to pay the court a £50 fee to make the request.

{{ partyName( 'claimant' ) }} can accept or refuse your request. If they refuse, the court will review the case.

What if I do nothing?

{{ partyName( 'claimant' ) }} can use enforcement options to recover the money. These include asking the court to:

  • send bailiffs to collect the money
  • take money out of your wages or any sale of land or property
