Get the company's full legal name right


Warning Your claim may fail if you don't use the company's full legal name.

The company's full legal name will be different from the brand name you know.

For example:

  • KFC is Kentucky Fried Chicken (Great Britain) Limited
  • Currys PC World is DSG Retail Limited
  • easyJet is easyJet Airline Company Limited
  • British Gas is British Gas Trading Limited
  • Saga Holidays is ST&H Group Limited

These names are just examples used to show the difference between a brand name and a full legal name. Don't assume they're correct or in date at the time you're making a claim.

How to find a company's full legal name

The full legal name will almost always end in 'limited', 'ltd' or 'plc'.

Try these steps to find it:

  1. Ask the company.
  2. Check your paperwork.
  3. Look online.
  4. Never use a trading name.

Ask the company

Talk to someone you dealt with or contact the company and ask what its full legal name is.

If your claim is connected to one branch of a chain, ask if the main company owns that branch or if it's a franchise.

A franchise is a branch that's not owned by its parent brand. For example, a branch of McDonalds or Starbucks might be owned by another company.

If you know you're claiming against a franchise, look for the full legal name of the company that owns the branch, not the main brand.

Check your paperwork

Look for the full legal name on any quotes, estimates, contracts, invoices, till receipts or documents that list terms and conditions.

You can often find it in the footer or in small print rather than in logos or headings.

View an example of a full legal name on a receipt

Look online

Go to the company's website and try to find the full legal name in terms and conditions or privacy policies.

You could also try entering the name you know the business by and 'terms and conditions' into a search engine.

Never use a trading name

If your paperwork says 'Saga Holidays is a trading name of ST&H Group Limited', then you should use 'ST&H Group Limited', the full legal name.
