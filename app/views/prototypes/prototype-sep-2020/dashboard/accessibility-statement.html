Accessibility statement for the Online Civil Money Claims service

This service allows you to make a money claim against an individual or an organisation for £10,000 or less.

This website is run by HM Courts and Tribunals Services (HMCTS). We want as many people as possible to be able to use it, so we’ve designed it to be accessible. For example, you should be able to:

  • change colours, contrast levels and fonts
  • zoom in up to 300% without the text spilling off the screen
  • navigate most of the website using just a keyboard
  • navigate most of the website using speech recognition software
  • listen to most of the website using a screen reader (including the most recent versions of JAWS, NVDA and VoiceOver)

We’ve also made the website text as simple as possible to understand.

AbilityNet (opens in a new window) has advice on making your device easier to use if you have a disability.

How accessible is this website?

This service was audited by the Digital Accessibility Centre (DAC) in January 2020. Except where stated below, all issues and recommendations from DAC have been implemented.

Enforcement procedure

The Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) is responsible for enforcing the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (the ‘accessibility regulations’). If you’re not happy with how we respond to your complaint, contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS) (opens in a new window).

Contacting us by phone or visiting us in person

We provide a text relay service for people who are D/deaf, hearing impaired or have a speech impediment. Our offices and Tribunal venues have audio induction loops, or if you contact us before your visit we can arrange a British Sign Language (BSL) interpreter. You can also request step-free access or a foreign language interpreter. Find out how to contact us (opens in a new window).

Technical information about this website’s accessibility

Online Civil Money Claims (OCMC) service is committed to making its website accessible, in accordance with the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018.

Compliance status

This website is partially compliant with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2.1 AA standard (opens in a new window), due to the non-compliances listed below.

Non-accessible content

Online Civil Money Claims (OCMC) service has been tested for accessibility. The content that is not accessible is outlined below with details of:

  • where it fails the success criteria
  • planned dates for when issues will be fixed

Some images within emails cannot be read by screen readers.
This fails WCAG 2.1A [1.1.1] success criterion (Non-text Content). We plan to fix this by October 2020.

Some links within emails cannot be read by screen readers.
This fails WCAG 2.1A [2.4.4] success criterion (Link Purpose in Context). We plan to fix this by October 2020.

Screen reader users were unable to identify the state of each task when navigating out of context in some of the pages. This fails WCAG 2.1A [1.3.1] success criterion (Info and Relationships). We plan to fix this by October 2020.

The order in which the table has been marked up is illogical in one of the pages. This fails WCAG 2.1A [1.3.2] success criterion (Meaningful Sequence). We plan to fix this by October 2020.

Error suggestions do not accurately describe the validation error that occurs and do not conform with GOV.UK Design System guidelines in one of the page. This fails WCAG 2.1AA [3.3.3] success criterion (Error Suggestion). We plan to fix this by October 2020.

One of the pages has a date picker element that is partially accessible to keyboard commands. This fails WCAG 2.1A [2.1.1] success criterion (Custom elements). We plan to fix this by October 2020.

Disproportionate burden

Links are present that are not descriptive out of context in one of the page. This fails WCAG 2.1AAA [2.4.9] success criterion (Link Purpose). We plan to fix this by October 2020.

Content that’s not within the scope of the accessibility regulations

Some PDFs, documents and email attachments published before 23 September 2018 might not meet accessibility standards, such as being screen reader compatible.

What we’re doing to improve accessibility

We are:

  • working on resolving any outstanding accessibility issues
  • planning to access unaudited service pages to ensure WCAG AA compliance

Preparation of this accessibility statement

This statement was prepared on 23 September 2019. It was last updated on 14 September 2020.

This website was last tested on 28 January 2020 by the Digital Accessibility Centre (DAC).
